What do I read?

A lot of things actually.

  • The book that needs no introduction. It serves as a chilling reminder even today of letting power over public life concentrate in few hands mainly due to ignorance, lack of participation and imagination of a different sort of world.

  • Written by Scott Galloway, a fierce critic of big tech, The Four - Facebook, Google, Amaxon, Apple - hold a lot of sway over people's lives today.
    A day does not go by when we do not interact with these companies.
    The author tears down the facade of goodness that they have, speaks about the effects they have on our world and how they crush competition and innovation while still looking innovative and good.

  • Nick Bostrom discusses the strategies on a high level to get to AGI(Artificial General Intelligence) and the dangers that intelligent machines possess such as superintelligence and improper goal orientation.

  • Martin Ford interviews many leaders in the field of AI including Geoffrey Hinton, Yoshua Bengio and Yann Lecunn to get their opinions on AI - the past, the present and the future.
    He discusses the dangers of AI such as wealth concentration and mass unemployment with everyone and everyone has their own unique opinions about everything.

  • Dr. Eric Topol is a renowned American cardiologist, the founder and director of the Scripps Research Translational Institute among other things.
    He has written 3 books on the future of medicine and healthcare. It was when he himself became a patient that he particularly realized the changes that can be made to medicine to help both patients and doctors.
